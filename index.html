Why Unapproved Devices Fail

Devices that skip DRAP registration skip the safety checks that keep patients safe. Sensors drift without traceable calibration, producing diagnostic readings that are silently wrong. Firmware ships untested, crashing mid-session and corrupting patient records.

In Pakistan's voltage-unstable grid, devices without IEC 60601 power testing fail unpredictably — sometimes with shock hazard to the patient. Unshielded electronics cause EMI that corrupts adjacent monitors in ICUs and operating theatres.

When a batch defect is found in an unregistered device, there is no recall mechanism. The dangerous units stay in use. DRAP registration exists precisely to prevent all of this — and we help manufacturers and facilities get there.

Technical Guidance
Common Faults in Unapproved Medical Devices

Medical devices that bypass DRAP approval lack verified safety testing. These are the most common failure modes — and why they happen.

Why Unapproved Devices Fail More Often

DRAP registration requires manufacturers to verify electromagnetic safety, sensor accuracy, firmware stability, biocompatibility, and post-market surveillance. Devices that skip this process skip these verifications. The faults listed below are direct consequences of that gap. If your device shows any of these signs, contact us immediately for an assessment.

All Diagnostic Devices
Sensor Drift — False Readings
Without factory calibration against a traceable standard, sensors drift over time. A glucometer, blood analyzer, or force meter may read 10–20% below or above actual values — silently, without any error message. Patients receive wrong diagnoses. Treatments are calibrated to wrong baselines.
Patient Safety Risk Silent Failure Calibration Gap
HIGH SEVERITY
Report this issue or request calibration →
Electronic Diagnostic Equipment
EMI / Electrical Interference
Unregistered devices are not tested for electromagnetic compatibility (EMC). In clinical environments — where multiple devices run simultaneously — EMI from poorly shielded electronics corrupts sensor signals, causes measurement spikes, and can interfere with adjacent critical devices like ventilators and cardiac monitors.
Critical in ICU/OT EMC Not Tested Signal Corruption
HIGH SEVERITY
Request EMC assessment →
Microcontroller-Based Devices
Firmware Instability & Crashes
DRAP software-as-medical-device (SaMD) requirements mandate software lifecycle documentation, V&V testing, and anomaly management. Without this, firmware is often undertested. Devices freeze mid-session, lose recorded data, produce corrupted output files, or reboot unexpectedly during a diagnostic procedure.
Data Loss Risk No V&V Testing Firmware Unvalidated
MEDIUM-HIGH SEVERITY
Request firmware audit →
Portable & Battery-Powered Devices
Power Supply Instability
Devices not tested to IEC 60601-1 electrical safety standards may use undersized voltage regulators, inadequate battery management ICs, or missing transient protection. In Pakistan's unstable grid environment, voltage fluctuations cause data corruption, incorrect readings, internal component damage, and in severe cases — electric shock hazard to patients or operators.
Shock Hazard IEC 60601 Gap Data Corruption
HIGH SEVERITY
Request electrical safety check →
IVD Reagents & Analyzers
Reagent Lot Variability
Counterfeit or unregistered diagnostic reagents are frequently relabeled from substandard batches. Without DRAP's lot-release testing requirement, reagent sensitivity and specificity vary between lots — causing a test that produces accurate results one week to systematically miss pathologies the next. This is one of the primary vectors of the Jan 2025 crisis flagged by the Pakistan Pharmacists Association.
Misdiagnosis Risk Counterfeit Concern No Lot Testing
HIGH SEVERITY
Verify your reagent suppliers →
Cloud-Connected Devices
Unsecured Data Transmission
Devices with WiFi or Bluetooth connectivity that were not assessed for cybersecurity may transmit patient data without encryption, use hardcoded credentials, or lack certificate pinning. Patient health records can be intercepted or tampered with. DRAP's SaMD guidelines require cybersecurity documentation for any device transmitting patient data.
Patient Data Breach No Encryption SaMD Non-Compliant
MEDIUM SEVERITY
Request cybersecurity audit →
Patient-Contact Devices
Biocompatibility Failure
Devices that contact patient skin or body fluids must be tested for biocompatibility under ISO 10993. Unregistered devices frequently use low-cost plastics, adhesives, or electrode materials with no cytotoxicity or sensitization testing. This can cause contact dermatitis, skin burns, or systemic allergic reactions — particularly in patients with compromised skin integrity.
Skin Reaction Risk ISO 10993 Gap Untested Materials
MEDIUM SEVERITY
Request biocompatibility review →
All Equipment
Environmental Sensitivity (Heat/Humidity)
Pakistan's climate — particularly in summer — exposes medical devices to temperatures above 45°C and high humidity. Devices not tested for operating environment (IEC 60068 environmental testing) fail prematurely: LCD screens delaminate, solder joints crack, electrolytic capacitors dry out, and sensor membranes degrade. Accuracy degrades unpredictably without any user warning.
Climate-Specific Risk No Env. Testing Premature Failure
LOW-MEDIUM SEVERITY
Request device assessment →
All Registered & Unregistered Devices
No Recall Mechanism
DRAP registration requires manufacturers to maintain a post-market surveillance system that enables field safety corrective actions (recalls). Unregistered devices have no such infrastructure — when a batch-level defect is discovered, there is no way to identify and retrieve affected units from the field. Dangerous devices remain in use indefinitely.
Systemic Risk No PMS System Recall Impossible
SYSTEMIC SEVERITY
Set up your PMS system →
All Devices
Inadequate or Missing Labeling / IFU
DRAP requires all medical devices to carry specific labeling information including intended use, contraindications, sterilization status, and device classification. Missing or incorrect labeling means clinical staff cannot make informed decisions about device use — leading to use outside intended parameters, use in contraindicated patients, and improper maintenance that accelerates device failure.
User Error Risk No IFU Labeling Non-Compliant
LOW-MEDIUM SEVERITY
Request labeling compliance check →
High-Power Devices
Leakage Current — Shock to Patient
IEC 60601-1 mandates that patient-applied parts (electrodes, probes) must not exceed 10µA leakage current under normal conditions and 50µA under single-fault conditions. Unapproved devices with poor isolation design may exceed these limits — causing micro-shocks to vulnerable patients, particularly those on cardiac monitors or in wet operating environments.
Electric Shock Risk IEC 60601 Violation Leakage Untested
CRITICAL SEVERITY
Request electrical safety testing →
Imaging & Signal Devices
Magnetic / RF Interference (MRI Areas)
Devices used near MRI suites or high-frequency surgical equipment must be MR-conditional or MR-unsafe labeled. Without this assessment, ferromagnetic components can become projectiles in MRI zones, and RF interference during electrosurgery can cause equipment malfunction or burns. DRAP requires MR safety documentation for devices intended for peri-MRI use.
Projectile Hazard MR Safety Gap Peri-MRI Risk
HIGH SEVERITY

Our DRAP Registration Consulting service covers every step of the registration process for medical devices in Pakistan:
